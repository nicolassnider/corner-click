# ITF Taekwondo Tournament — Sparring Business Rules
> Source: ITF Official Competition Rules, Version 2026-1

---

## 1. Competitor Eligibility

**BR-001** A competitor must be registered in exactly one club/school per event. Competing for more than one club in the same event is prohibited.

**BR-002** A competitor must compete in their assigned age category. They may not simultaneously compete in a higher or lower age category.

**BR-003** A competitor must compete in their assigned rank category (color belt / black belt). Cross-rank entry is not permitted.

**BR-004** All competitors must pass the official weigh-in. The first weigh-in occurs within 24 hours before competition start. A competitor who fails the first weigh-in has one (1) additional hour to present for a second and final weigh-in. Failure to make weight eliminates the competitor from the sparring division and all other weight categories.

**BR-005** A competitor/team called to their ring must present within one (1) minute of the announcement. Non-appearance within that window results in disqualification.

---

## 2. Match Duration and Structure

**BR-006** Individual elimination and final matches (World Championship) consist of two (2) rounds of two (2) minutes each, with a one (1) minute rest between rounds.

**BR-007** Individual Color Belt elimination matches (World Cup) consist of one (1) round of two (2) minutes.

**BR-008** Individual Black Belt elimination matches (World Cup) consist of one (1) round of three (3) minutes.

**BR-009** Individual Color Belt finals (World Cup) consist of one (1) round of two (2) minutes.

**BR-010** Individual Black Belt finals (World Cup) consist of two (2) rounds of two (2) minutes with a one (1) minute rest between rounds.

**BR-011** Team matches consist of one (1) round of two (2) minutes per bout.

---

## 3. Scoring

**BR-012** One (1) point is awarded for any legal hand attack to the middle or upper section.

**BR-013** Two (2) points are awarded for any legal foot attack to the middle section.

**BR-014** Three (3) points are awarded for any legal foot attack to the upper section.

**BR-015** A technique is valid under the Light Contact system only if it:
- Is executed correctly (proper form and tool).
- Is dynamic (force, purpose, speed, and accuracy).
- Is controlled on the target.

---

## 4. Legal Target Areas

**BR-016** Legal head targets are the front, sides, and top of the head. The back of the head and the neck are excluded.

**BR-017** Legal body targets are the front torso from shoulder to navel vertically, and from the armpit line to the waist on each side. The back is excluded.

---

## 5. Point Deductions (Gam-jeom)

The following infractions result in one (1) point being deducted from the offending competitor's score:

**BR-018** Hard contact (as defined in the ITF Glossary).

**BR-019** Attacking a downed opponent.

**BR-020** Leg sweep.

**BR-021** Grabbing.

**BR-022** Intentional attack to an illegal target area (any area outside T33).

**BR-023** Unsportsmanlike conduct, including: failure to follow referee instructions, intentionally leaving the ring to manipulate time, inappropriate behavior or language toward referees, officials, or other competitors.

**BR-024** Three (3) accumulated warnings automatically trigger one (1) point deduction.

---

## 6. Warnings

The following infractions result in a warning:

**BR-025** Simulating a scored point by raising one or both arms.

**BR-026** Stepping completely outside the ring (both feet outside). Exception: if the competitor was intentionally pushed out without performing a technique, no warning is issued to them; the pusher receives the warning instead.

**BR-027** Falling (any part of the body other than the feet touching the ground), whether intentional or accidental.

**BR-028** Simulating a strike or injury to gain advantage.

**BR-029** Intentionally avoiding combat.

**BR-030** Adjusting equipment during the match without the Central Referee's consent.

**BR-031** Unintentional attack to an illegal target area.

**BR-032** Pushing with hands, shoulders, or body.

---

## 7. Disqualification

**BR-033** A competitor is disqualified for any of the following:

| Code | Infraction |
|------|-----------|
| DQ-01 | Misconduct toward referees or ignoring their instructions |
| DQ-02 | Excessive or uncontrolled contact |
| DQ-03 | Accumulating three (3) direct point deductions imposed by the Central Referee |
| DQ-04 | Being under the influence of alcohol or drugs |
| DQ-05 | Loss of temper |
| DQ-06 | Insulting an opponent, coach, or referee (results in disqualification from the entire event) |
| DQ-07 | Biting or scratching |
| DQ-08 | Attacking with the knee, elbow, or forehead |
| DQ-09 | Being found guilty of causing loss of consciousness in the opponent |

---

## 8. Injury Protocol

**BR-034** When a competitor is injured, the Central Referee stops the match and calls the Medical Officer. A total of three (3) minutes of medical time is allowed per match.

**BR-035** The medical time countdown starts when the Medical Officer is in front of the injured competitor and stops when they leave. If the Medical Officer is called again in the same match, the clock resumes from where it last stopped.

**BR-036** A competitor requiring more than three (3) cumulative minutes of medical attention during a match cannot continue and loses the match.

**BR-037** Regardless of remaining medical time, a concussion examination must last at least two (2) minutes.

**BR-038** If the injured competitor cannot continue:
- They win if the opponent is at fault.
- They lose if the opponent is not at fault.

**BR-039** If both competitors are injured simultaneously and neither can continue, the competitor with more points at that moment is declared the winner. If tied, the Ring Council decides.

**BR-040** A competitor diagnosed with a concussion is removed from the entire event and cannot compete in any remaining matches.

**BR-041** A competitor who refuses to accept the Medical Officer's or Ring Council's decision is disqualified and removed from the competition.

---

## 9. Tie-Breaking

**BR-042** In case of a tie at the end of regulation, one (1) additional minute is contested.

**BR-043** If still tied after the extra round, the first point scored and confirmed simultaneously by at least two (2) referees decides the winner (sudden death).

**BR-044** In team events, if the 5-bout result is a tie, each coach selects one (1) competitor for a single deciding bout of two (2) minutes.

**BR-045** If that deciding bout also ends in a tie, sudden death applies (first point confirmed by at least two referees wins).

**BR-046** In tie-break rounds, previously accumulated warnings and/or point deductions do not carry over.

---

## 10. Match Procedure

**BR-047** Competitors start at their designated positions wearing red or blue hand/foot/head protectors, plus mandatory transparent mouthguard.

**BR-048** Before the match starts, competitors bow in sequence to the Jury table, the Central Referee, and each other.

**BR-049** The Central Referee starts the bout with "SIJAK" and stops it with "HAECHYO". Competitors must stop immediately and remain in place until restarted.

**BR-050** Time is continuous. The Central Referee may stop the clock with "JUNG-JI" and resume with "GAE-SOK".

**BR-051** A competitor who leaves the ring must restart one (1) meter inside the ring.

**BR-052** A final horn signal ends scoring. The Central Referee closes the round/match, reverse bowing is performed, and the result is announced.

---

## 11. Equipment Replacement

**BR-053** Defective or contaminated equipment may be replaced within three (3) minutes. Failure to replace it within that window results in disqualification and award of match points to the opponent.

---

## 12. Draw and Seeding

**BR-054** Bracket positions and all byes are determined by random computer draw.

**BR-055** Previous edition's top-3 finishers in the same category are seeded at the top and bottom of the bracket.

**BR-056** The draw is published at least 48 hours before the event.

**BR-057** A minimum of four (4) competitors or teams must be registered in a division for medals to count toward overall trophies.

---

## 13. Team-Specific Rules

**BR-058** A coin toss between the two coaches determines which team sends their first competitor to the ring. Teams alternate after that.

**BR-059** All five (5) bouts of a team match must be completed. If a competitor withdraws due to injury or to gain a tactical advantage, the opponent is declared winner and four (4) points are added to the opposing team's total score.

**BR-060** Team rosters (5 competitors + 1 optional reserve) must be submitted to the Jury President immediately before the division starts and before each subsequent bout.

**BR-061** Once the team roster is submitted, no substitutions are allowed for that division.

**BR-062** Any registered competitor matching the team's age and gender category may be selected for a team event, even if not originally listed as a team member — subject to roster lock rules in BR-061.

**BR-063** Team roster changes are allowed up to 60 minutes before the scheduled time of the category. After that deadline, the locked roster stands.

---

## 14. Officials

**BR-064** The active Ring Council for a sparring match consists of:
- 1 Jury President
- 1 Jury Member
- 1 IT Referee
- 1 Central Referee
- 4 Corner Referees
- 2 Equipment Checkers

**BR-065** Any potential disqualification under the injury protocol (T39) must be reviewed with a member of the Referees Committee before a final decision is issued.
